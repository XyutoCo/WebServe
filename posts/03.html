Guide to the best Chrome Extensions!

Author: Kit Walker | Date: 25 May 2015

Chrome in my opinion is the best browser out there. As a web designer for me the main benefit of Chrome is the developer console. It makes looking at other peoples code easy and whats-more you can download lots of widgets that make chrome much more useful. In this short post I will cover some of the most useful extensions that I use, in no particular order.

1. Ad Blocker

Although I think that for some people the Adsense scheme is really important for their livelihood but I like to pay instead of torrenting or the like. Furthermore I occasionally donate to the developer and I like to ensure that they have the money to fund there future endeavours.

2. WhatFont

As a developer of websites and the like I am always looking at the different colours and fonts that other sites use and so WhatFont enables me to quickly search what fonts they are using and so I can ask the creator for the rights to use a font.


3. ColourZilla

ColourZilla much like WhatFont is used for getting colour values from the internet so I can re use them myself. I do occasionally use the built in colour Meter on OSX but ColourZilla is good because it copies the value straight to the clipboard.
