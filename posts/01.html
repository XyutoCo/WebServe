Pros and Cons of Overclocking a CPU

Author: Callum Potter | Date: 16 June 2015

The Asus rampage line from the republic of gamers has recently been put under my radar as the best motherboard on the current commercial market. Whilst the heavy price drags it down if you’re looking to break record this is recommended for you.

One of its most valuable qualities has to be the detail put into the thing they call “the overclocking armoury”. The special designed Haswell 22nm chip boards provide very well designed and fast power. In my other blog I mentioned about the risk of overclocking processors well the rampage has extra sets of pins which means the voltage load of the cpu it drastically change meaning there is little to no change in voltage making overclocking a lot safer. With the supplied oc panel this overclocking becomes the easiest it can be. The OC panel allows for alterations to fanRPM, instant clock change and a display of the processors temperature to ensure it isn’t damaged. With its own inbuilt sound card and Ethernet connecter both with extremely promising specification’s and heck even the card is made of nickel to stop static build up and the layout of RMA is designed to maximise output and 20x faster than SSD.

However of course this isn’t going to be cheap. Starting retail prices at lowest are around £350-£500 which may sound a lot for a simple motherboard but trust me for what it’s worth I would defiantly recommend this.
