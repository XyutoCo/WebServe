Finding the IP address of an Apple Mac

Author: Kit Walker | Date: 19 January 2016

The IP address for a computer is an address that identifies any LAN enabled device on a network. You may want to find your IP for a number of reasons:
  • Setting up a static IP.
  • File sharing, via samba.
  • Port forwarding for remote access.
Here listed below are a few ways to identify your IP address:
  1. System Preferences.
    • Open the network tab of system preferences by opening Spotlight (CMD + Space)
    • Search Network and open:
    • In this tab, you will find a sentence saying your IP in both the Ethernet and Wi-Fi tab:
  2. Fing.
    Fing is an app that can be found on both IOS and Android, it helps you search your network for all of the devices. Then look on the app for your device name.
    • Search in the AppStore for "Fing" and download this app:
    • Open the app and search, then locate your PC and find it's Ip.
